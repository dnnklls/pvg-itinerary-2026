Evening

Shanghai Tower, at dusk.Book ahead

Go up around 5:30. The 118th-floor deck catches the city turning from daylight to a field of lights near seven — sunset is the best hour of the whole stay.

Booking, the practical version: grab it on Trip.com or Klook (from ~¥160). Bring your passport — it can be checked at entry. Don't book too far ahead: if it's grey or pouring, skip it, the view isn't worth it. Screenshot the QR ticket in case you're offline.

If things change

Plan B, C, and rain.

The plan above assumes a decent evening and some energy. When neither is true, drop down a level — guilt-free.

If it's pouring

Plum-rain season means sudden downpours. Stay dry and indoors.

  • Skip the waterfront walk.
  • Go covered: IFC, Super Brand Mall, or Kerry Parkside near base.
  • Only do Shanghai Tower if visibility's decent — clouds kill the view.
  • Dinner in Lujiazui or back by the hotel.

If you're tired (Plan B)

The red-eye hit, but you've still got something left.

  • Long lie-in, late-afternoon nap.
  • Crew happy hour, then the hotel pool and gym.
  • Sunset stroll in Century Park, twelve minutes away.
  • Early dinner at Kerry Parkside. In bed by nine.

If you're completely destroyed (Plan C)

Some red-eyes just win. Don't fight it — protect the sleep.

  • Don't leave Pudong. At all.
  • Century Park, Kerry Parkside, a hotel massage, early dinner.
  • No Bund. No tower. Save it for the next PVG.

Before you land

Logistics.

Phone and apps

Google, WhatsApp, and Instagram are blocked. Turn on roaming or an eSIM — roaming data bypasses the firewall — or have a VPN ready before you land. Use Apple Maps or Amap, and Didi for rides.

Paying for things

Near-cashless city. Link a card to Alipay or WeChat Pay before arrival — most stalls and taxis won't take foreign cards. Keep a little cash as backup.

Late-June weather

Plum-rain season: hot, sticky, 28–32°C with sudden downpours. Pack a compact umbrella and light, breathable clothes. See the rain plan above if it turns.

Getting around

Metro Line 7 is at the door — add the metro QR in Alipay. Didi is cheap and works in English via the Alipay mini-program. Station rule: metered taxis only, and don't drink the tap water.

Crew perks

Happy hour 1–6pm, 25% off food and drink with crew ID, free shuttle to Sanchenli Mall at 11:00 and 18:20, and the Lobby Bar crew lounge. The front desk handles your wake-up call.
